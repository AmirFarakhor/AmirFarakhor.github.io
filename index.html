Amir Farakhor

Welcome to my website!

Hello there! I'm Amir, a passionate researcher exploring the dynamic intersections of power and energy systems. Holding a Ph.D. in Power Electronics from the University of Tabriz since February 2019, my academic journey also includes B.Sc. and M.Sc. degrees in Electrical Power Engineering from Azarbaijan Shahid Madani University, Tabriz, Iran.

Currently, I'm in the world of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Kansas, pursuing a Ph.D. in advanced battery energy storage management systems. My goal is to fortify the future of Lithium-Ion battery technology, ensuring resilience against faults and enhancing user safety. My expertise spans multiple domains, weaving together electrical engineering, energy systems and power electronics, with mechanical engineering, battery energy storage systems and control theory. This diverse background shapes me into a well-rounded researcher and engineer. I am intereted in a wide range reserach spectrum, ranging from renewable energy integration, power electronics, and transportation electrification to control theory, machine learning, and optimal control. Beyond academia, I enjoy working on PLC automation and embedded systems coding.

Away from the research, I'm a nature enthusiast and a sports lover. I play chess, basketball, and table tennis to maintain a healthy balance. I enjoy working collaboratively, and I'm always ready to lend a hand or engage in discussions.

Feel free to explore my website and connect with me!
